Why Exterior Painting Matters

Exterior house painting is more than just a fresh coat of color; it plays a crucial role in protecting your home from the elements. A properly applied paint job shields surfaces from harmful UV rays, moisture buildup, and fluctuating temperatures, which can cause cracks, peeling, and long-term structural damage. By investing in high-quality paints like Sherwin-Williams SuperPaint or Duration, you ensure durability, enhanced curb appeal, and increased property value. Whether you live in Manteno, Momence, or any surrounding area, a well-maintained exterior sets your home apart and makes a lasting impression.

Step-by-Step Guide to Painting Your Home’s Exterior

  • Evaluate the Surface: Start by thoroughly inspecting your home’s exterior for any visible cracks, mold growth, or peeling paint. Cleaning the surface with a pressure washer removes dirt, mildew, and old paint, ensuring a smooth foundation for the new coat.
  • Choose the Right Paint: Selecting the appropriate paint type is essential. Use Sherwin-Williams SuperPaint for wooden surfaces, Loxon for stucco and masonry, and DTM (Direct-To-Metal) for metal exteriors. This ensures strong adhesion and long-lasting protection.
  • Prep the Surface: Sand rough areas, caulk any gaps to prevent water intrusion, and apply a high-quality primer if necessary. Proper preparation is the key to achieving a smooth, even finish.
  • Check the Weather: Painting in optimal weather conditions is crucial. Aim for temperatures between 50°F and 85°F, avoiding direct sunlight and ensuring no rain is forecast for at least 24 hours.
  • Apply the Paint: Begin with detailed areas like trim and edges using a high-quality brush. For large surfaces, use a roller or sprayer for even coverage. Applying at least two coats will provide maximum durability and vibrancy.
  • Finishing Touches: Once the paint is dry, carefully remove any painter’s tape, touch up missed spots, and thoroughly clean your brushes, rollers, and sprayers for future use.
